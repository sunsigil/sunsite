DIFFICULTIES

The thing I had the most trouble with in this project was definitely collision resolution. Detection went about as planned besides a few minor hiccups that were worked out quickly, and while getting everything with rendering, interaction, and scene management set up was a lot of work but it was expected work that made sense. The first problem with collision resolution was determining the collision point and normal for circle-AABB collision. As discussed in class, the case where the circle's center is outside the box is easy. But when the circle's center is inside the box, you've got to do some shenanigans with deducing the nearest edge to the circle's center. Once you've got that though, you're all good. After that was cleared up, frankly, I suffered from a severe lack of reading the slides. If I had just looked at the formulas for determining post-collision velocities more closely both in my own old code and in the slides I would have spent much less time getting my collision to look satisfactorily natural. When I was just winging it according to how I thought it should work, there were a few annoying mystery-cases where balls would just not act how I knew they should.
